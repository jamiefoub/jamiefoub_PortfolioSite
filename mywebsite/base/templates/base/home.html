4th Year Project

DESIGN OF A CROSS DIE FOR SHEET METAL FORMING PROCESS

Previous Next
Introduction

I completed my 4th year individual project on the “DESIGN OF A CROSS DIE FOR SHEET METAL FORMING PROCESS”. In which I applied my Solid Works knowledge to produce CAD models and accompanying component drawings of the complete die design. I was also required to learn and implement new skills in Abaqus FEA software to compute simulation testing of the cross die. I achieved an “A” grade for this work.

Project Summary

When working with sheet metal it is critical that the engineer has a good representation of the formability limits of a given material. This can be achieved using a materials Forming Limit Curve (FLC). This is an essential tool for the engineer when designing sheet metal parts.

Currently the two most popular testing methods for producing a materials FLC are the Nakazima and Marciniak tests. Though these testing methods are proven to be very popular and effective ways in producing the FLC, they do have some drawbacks.

These testing methods require multiple tests to be carried out with material samples of varying sizes and geometries. This is required to account for the full range of the failure modes of the FLC. This proves to be very time consuming and expensive due to materials waste and iterative testing that is required.

The Solution to these problems could be solved through the use of cross-die testing. The cross-die testing method is capable of comprising a broad range of stress-strain states. This effectively allows the cross-die in a single test to achieve the same amount of data as several iterations of the Nakazima and Marciniak tests. Significantly cutting down on material waste, cost and testing time.

Project Aims and Objectives
  • Study background theory of sheet metal forming techniques to provide an understanding as to the purpose of sheet metal forming
  • Understand current sheet metal testing techniques and their possible shortcomings
  • Provide theory on Forming Limit Diagrams (Forming Limit Curve)
  • Provide theory on cross-die testing and how it is an effective testing method
  • Showcase final cross-die design
  • Simulate cross dies testing in ABAQUS FEA

5th Year Project

DESIGN OF BLAST AND FIRE WALL FOR NEW BUILD FPSO

Previous Next
Introduction

For my 5th year group project, we were tasked with designing a fire and blast wall to separate modules on a new build FPSO, whilst optimising for a low cost and low weight design. This would provide protection between modules in the event of an explosion and accompanying jet fire.

My main contributions to the group were developing design optimisation code using MATLAB as well as producing the groups CAD models and drawings using Solid Works. I also carried out the finite element analysis of the groups final blast wall design using Abaqus software. I was graded an “A” for this work.

Project Summary

The aim for this project was to present a detailed design solution for a fire and blast wall for use between modules on a new process area of a floating production, storage, and offloading (FPSO) unit. The blast wall was required to be able to withstand extremely harsh working environments as the FPSO will be operating in arctic conditions, resulting in sub-zero temperatures and harsh corrosion.

The fire and blast wall was designed for an idealised isosceles triangle pressure pulse with peak over pressure of 2.5 bar and duration of 20 milliseconds. The wall was also required to have a fire rating of J120 following international standard testing ISO 22899-1. This requires the wall to be able to withstand a jet fire for 120 minutes.

The detailed wall design was developed through the use of the single degree of freedom (SDOF) method as outlined in FABIG’s technical note. This method was then implemented into an optimisation code using Matlab, that produced an optimal low weight design. Abaqus FEA software was then used to verify these results whilst producing a more detailed analysis into the response of the blast wall. Thus, providing a detailed fire and blast wall design solution.

Project Aims and Objectives
  • Design a fire and blast wall to meet customer specification
  • Work with FABIG’s technical notes to help guide our design
  • Carry out material selection to withstand harsh corrosive environment and meet fire rating
  • Develop MatLab code to optimise for the low weight and low cost design
  • Build on Abaqus FEA and SolidWorks CAD knowledge
  • Work as team throughout all stages of the project
